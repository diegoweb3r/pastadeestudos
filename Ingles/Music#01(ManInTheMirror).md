| A summer's disregard | Descaso ou falta de atenção

## History 
Written by Garrett e Ballard in 1987, they showed to Quincy Jone, who was working in Michael Jackson's new album, Bad. Jackson loved the song because the message of the lyrics matched with the theme that he wanted to show in his work.    
The inspiration came from Ballard himself, who wanted to create a music with a strong message about the need to make an internal change to change the world.    
Michael recorded the song with the support of a gospel choir, including one of the writers, Garrett. Released in 1988 as the fourth single of Bad. Even though it wasn't the first choice for a single, became a huge success.
